## Preferred VS Common Stocks
Owners of preferred stocks have a higher claim over distributions than commons stock holders. They trade this for their voting rights in corporate governance

**Distributions** refer to dividends and returning money in case of liquidation. Preferred stock holders have priority in receiving these.

# Derivatives
From *Hull*: "A derivative can be defined as a financial instrument whose value depends on (or derives from) the values of other, more basic, underlying variables"

## Forward & spot contracts
Forward contract is a conteract to exchange an asset for an agreed upon price at a specified future date. In contrast, Spot contract is an exchange which happens immediately. 

### Long vs Short Positions
There are 2 positions in a trade: A *long* and *short* position.
At the time of the trade, the party *purchasing the asset* is in a long position, while the party *selling the asset* is in a short position

* **Long** - Buying with the expectation of price going up
* **Short** - Selling with the expectation of the price going down

So in a forward contract, the party buying the asset is in a long position, the seller is in the short position.

#### Note:
The derivative here is not the underlying asset that will be traded at the time of maturity of the contract. The derivative **is the contract**. While the terms of the contract are the same, e.g. 100 kilos of mangos will be sold at a certain date at a certain price, the asset here being traded *is the forward contract*. The contract has a price of its own.

## Futures

Futures are just like forward contracts. Forward contracts are traded over the counter, while Futures are traded on exchanges
