# Meanings
Bid Price refers to the price that a *buyer is willing to pay*
Ask Price is the price a *seller is willing to sell for*
Bid-Ask Spread is the price difference between the prevailing bid and ask prices in a market for an asset

# In markets
The bid price is the amount of money a buyer is willing to pay for a security. It is contrasted with the sell (ask or offer) price, which is the amount a seller is willing to sell a security for.

Limit Bid and Ask orders enforce the price at which to buy / sell a security. However, if I choose a market bid order, the exchange wants to match me to the market rate, i.e. it will make me buy at the ask price, so that I meet the sellers' demands.

## How do market makers profit from spread
Market makers allow for *much quicker* stock transactions in a market than would happen if buyers were interacting directly with sellers. How do they do this? Consider just one stock, stock A. 
* Say a buyer wants to buy 100 of A, and a seller wants to sell 100 of A
* Rather than transferring stocks from seller to buyer, and money from buyer to seller, The market maker simultaneously:
    * Buys 100 from the seller
    * And sells to 100 the buyer
* So this way, the transaction has been completed much quicker and easier for the buyer and seller

If there is a spread, i.e. ask > bid, then the market maker can buy from the seller at bid price (lower), and sell to the buyer at ask price (higher) $\rightarrow$ Profit.

## NBBO
NBBO, the National Best Bid and Offer prices for a security, are the best prices taken from across all exchanges where the security is listed.

# Level 2 Quotes
These display more than just the NBBO: they display the top 40, top 80, top 200 bid and ask prices for a security. They are used to gauge *market depth*. These quotes also show the corresponding size for each offer.
